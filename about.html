About Sabah - The Land Below the Wind

Sabah is Malaysia's easternmost state, located on the northern portion of the island of Borneo. Known as "Negeri Di Bawah Bayu" (Land Below the Wind), Sabah lies south of the typhoon belt, making it a year-round tropical paradise.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Diversity of Indigenous Peoples

Sabah is home to over 32 indigenous ethnic groups, each with its own unique traditions, languages, and customs. The largest groups include the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, and Murut peoples. This rich tapestry of cultures is celebrated through vibrant festivals, traditional music and dance, and exquisite handicrafts.

Visitors can experience authentic cultural immersion through community-based tourism programs, where you can stay in traditional longhouses, participate in daily activities, and learn about ancient customs passed down through generations.

Traditional Festivals

  1. Harvest Festival (Kaamatan): Celebrated in May by the Kadazan-Dusun people to give thanks for the rice harvest
  2. Regatta Lepa: A colorful Bajau water festival featuring traditional sailing boats
  3. Pesta Kalimaran: Murut cultural festival showcasing traditional games and ceremonies
  4. Sabah Fest: A grand cultural celebration held annually in Kota Kinabalu

Sabah Cuisine

Flavors of Borneo

Sabah's cuisine reflects its multicultural heritage, blending Malay, Chinese, and indigenous influences. Fresh seafood is a highlight, with fish, prawns, and squid prepared in countless delicious ways. Don't miss trying hinava (Kadazan-Dusun fish salad), bambangan (wild mango), and the famous Sabah seaweed.

Night markets in Kota Kinabalu offer an incredible variety of street food, from grilled satay to fresh seafood noodles. For adventurous foodies, local markets offer exotic fruits like durian, tarap, and rambutan when in season.

Incredible Biodiversity

Sabah boasts some of the richest biodiversity on Earth. Its ancient rainforests are home to an astonishing variety of flora and fauna, many found nowhere else in the world.

🌿

Conservation Efforts

Sabah has designated approximately 50% of its land area as protected forests and wildlife reserves. Organizations like the Sabah Wildlife Department and WWF Malaysia work tirelessly to protect endangered species including orangutans, pygmy elephants, and sun bears.

Key Wildlife Species

  • Borneo Orangutan: The "old man of the forest" - critically endangered and iconic
  • Proboscis Monkey: Unique to Borneo with its distinctive large nose
  • Pygmy Elephant: The smallest elephant species in the world
  • Sun Bear: The world's smallest bear species
  • Rafflesia: The world's largest flower, found in Sabah's forests
