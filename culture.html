Culture & Heritage

Rich Cultural Tapestry of Sabah

Sabah is a melting pot of cultures, home to over 32 indigenous ethnic groups, each with its own unique traditions, languages, and customs. From the majestic longhouses of the Rungus people to the vibrant water festivals of the Bajau, Sabah's cultural heritage is as diverse as its natural landscapes. Explore the living traditions that have been passed down through generations and experience the warm hospitality of Sabah's people.

Indigenous Ethnic Groups

Sabah's population is a beautiful mosaic of indigenous peoples who have called this land home for centuries. Each group contributes unique elements to the state's rich cultural identity.

🏔️

Kadazan-Dusun

The largest ethnic group in Sabah, known for their rice cultivation, traditional costumes, and the famous Harvest Festival (Kaamatan).

🌊

Bajau

Often called "Sea Gypsies," the Bajau are master horsemen and skilled fishermen, famous for their Regatta Lepa festival.

🎯

Murut

Known as the "Hill People," the Murut are celebrated for their blowpipe hunting skills, traditional long jumps, and vibrant Pesta Kalimaran.

🏠

Rungus

Famous for their beautifully decorated longhouses and intricate beadwork, the Rungus preserve ancient traditions in Kudat.

🎭

Lundayeh

Known for their unique bamboo musical instruments and traditional rice wine (tapai), the Lundayeh live in the highlands.

🌺

Bisaya

Coastal dwellers known for their vibrant water festivals, traditional boat-making, and delicious seafood cuisine.

Traditional Festivals

Sabah's calendar is filled with colorful festivals that celebrate harvests, cultural heritage, and community spirit. These events offer visitors a chance to witness authentic traditions and join in the celebrations.

Kaamatan - Harvest Festival

When: May 30-31

The most important festival for the Kadazan-Dusun people, celebrating the rice harvest with traditional dances, buffalo races, and the crowning of the Harvest Festival Queen (Unduk Ngadau).

Regatta Lepa

When: April

A spectacular Bajau water festival in Semporna featuring beautifully decorated traditional sailing boats (lepa), cultural performances, and sea sports competitions.

Pesta Kalimaran

When: March

The Murut cultural festival showcasing traditional games, blowpipe competitions, cultural dances, and the unique ritual of Magunatip (bamboo dance).

🎨 Traditional Arts & Crafts

Intricate Beadwork

Sabah's indigenous communities are renowned for their exquisite beadwork, creating colorful necklaces, bracelets, and decorative items. Each pattern and color combination carries cultural significance and tells stories of the community's heritage.

Traditional Weaving

The Rungus and Dusun people produce beautiful woven textiles using backstrap looms. These fabrics, known as sinulaman, feature geometric patterns and are used in traditional costumes and ceremonial dress.

Bamboo Crafts & Musical Instruments

Bamboo is a versatile material used to create everything from musical instruments like the sompoton (mouth organ) and tongkungon (bamboo zither) to household items and decorative crafts.

Cultural Etiquette Tips

  • Always ask permission before taking photos of people or inside longhouses
  • Remove shoes when entering traditional homes
  • Dress modestly when visiting villages and religious sites
  • Accept food and drinks offered as a sign of hospitality
  • Learn a few basic greetings in the local language - it goes a long way!
