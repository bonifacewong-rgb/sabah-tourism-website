Local Food & Cuisine

Flavors of Borneo

Sabah's cuisine is a delightful fusion of Malay, Chinese, and indigenous influences, creating a unique culinary identity that reflects the state's multicultural heritage. From fresh seafood caught daily in the South China Sea to exotic fruits found only in Borneo's rainforests, Sabah offers an unforgettable gastronomic adventure. Whether you're dining at a high-end restaurant in Kota Kinabalu or sampling street food at a night market, every meal tells a story of the land and its people.

Must-Try Sabah Dishes

No visit to Sabah is complete without tasting these iconic local dishes:

Kadazan-Dusun

Hinava

A refreshing raw fish salad made with fresh mackerel, lime juice, shallots, ginger, and chili. Sabah's answer to ceviche!

~ RM 15-25

Chinese-Sabahan

Tuaran Mee

Handmade egg noodles from Tuaran town, stir-fried with char siu (BBQ pork), vegetables, and egg. A Sabah breakfast staple.

~ RM 8-15

Malay

Soto Sabah

A comforting chicken or beef soup with rice cakes, bean sprouts, and a rich, aromatic broth flavored with turmeric and lemongrass.

~ RM 7-12

Seafood

Fresh Grilled Seafood

Choose your catch at the Filipino Market or seafood restaurants. Tiger prawns, lobsters, and fish grilled to perfection with sambal.

~ RM 30-80

Indigenous

Bambangan

A wild mango unique to Borneo, typically pickled and served as a tangy side dish with rice. A true taste of the rainforest!

~ RM 5-10

Nyonya

Sabah Laksa

A coconut-based curry laksa with thick rice noodles, prawns, chicken, and tofu puffs. Rich, creamy, and full of flavor.

~ RM 8-15

Sabah Night Markets - A Food Lover's Paradise

Sabah's night markets are where the real culinary magic happens. As the sun sets, streets come alive with sizzling woks, grilling satay, and the aroma of spices. The most famous is the Filipino Market (Pasar Besar Kota Kinabalu), where you can pick fresh seafood and have it cooked on the spot. Don't miss the Gaya Street Sunday Market for local snacks, fruits, and handicrafts. For an authentic experience, visit the Api-Api Night Food Market where locals gather for affordable, delicious meals.

Popular night market foods include grilled chicken wings, satay skewers, roti canai (flaky flatbread), kuih cincin (ring-shaped cookies), and refreshing ais kacang (shaved ice dessert). The atmosphere is lively, the food is cheap, and the flavors are unforgettable!

Try Making Hinava at Home!

🐟 Traditional Hinava Recipe

Ingredients: 300g fresh mackerel (or any white fish), 5 limes, 1 red onion (sliced), 2 bird's eye chilies, 1-inch ginger (julienned), salt to taste.

Instructions:

  1. Finely dice the fresh fish into small cubes.
  2. Squeeze lime juice over the fish and let it "cook" in the acid for 10 minutes.
  3. Drain excess lime juice and mix in sliced onions, chilies, and ginger.
  4. Season with salt to taste.
  5. Serve chilled with steamed rice and sliced cucumber.

Tip: The fresher the fish, the better! This dish is best enjoyed within a few hours of preparation.

Dining Tips for Visitors

  • Most local eateries are cash-only - bring small bills
  • Breakfast favorites: Tuaran Mee, Roti Canai, Kolo Mee
  • Ask for "kurang pedas" if you prefer less spicy food
  • Seafood is freshest and cheapest at the Filipino Market
  • Try "teh tarik" (pulled milk tea) - Malaysia's national drink
